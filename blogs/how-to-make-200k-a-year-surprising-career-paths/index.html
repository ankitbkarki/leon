How to Make $200K a Year: Surprising Paths You Might Not Expect

BY Leon PUBLISHED ON May 3, 2025

Let’s be real for a second — making $200K a year is a huge milestone. You’d probably assume that to hit that kind of salary, you’d have to be a doctor, a high-level executive, or maybe even some kind of tech wizard, right? Well, yes, those paths still exist, but honestly, there are more unexpected routes than you might think. It’s not always about climbing the corporate ladder or getting a degree from a top-tier school. Sometimes, it’s about finding the right industry, the right niche, and maybe even taking a risk or two. So, how do you actually make $200K a year in 2025? Let’s dive into it.

  • Sales: The Underestimated High-Earner
  • The Surprising Path: Trades and Small Business Ownership

  • Final Thoughts: It’s Possible, But It’s Not Easy

The Obvious Ones: Tech and Finance

Let’s start with the classic — tech and finance. These industries have been the go-to for high-paying jobs for years, and there’s a reason for that. Software engineers, data scientists, and cybersecurity professionals are still pulling in impressive salaries. But, I think we’re all aware of that, right? It’s the ones that don’t fit the mold that I find more interesting.

But back to tech for a moment — a senior-level engineer, especially one with experience in things like cloud computing or machine learning, can easily cross the $200K threshold. And don’t forget the perks. Stock options, signing bonuses, and performance bonuses can really bump up those numbers.

In finance, it’s much the same story. I mean, just think about people working in private equity or hedge funds. Sure, the work is intense, and you’re likely working long hours, but for many, it’s more than worth it. It’s about the mix of salary and bonuses, the long-term rewards for short-term sacrifices.

Sales: The Underestimated High-Earner

Now, let’s talk about sales. You probably didn’t expect to see that one on the list, did you? But hear me out. If you’re selling something like enterprise software or high-end consulting services, you can easily hit $200K or more, especially if your commissions are tied to performance.

I mean, one Redditor shared their story about tech sales, saying they made $120K base salary, but their commissions alone pushed them well over the $200K mark. That’s a pretty solid incentive to close deals, right? Of course, it’s not always guaranteed — sales jobs can be high pressure, and you’ve got to hit your numbers to see the big payout. Still, if you’ve got a knack for building relationships and closing big deals, it’s a career that could be your ticket to a hefty paycheck.

The Surprising Path: Trades and Small Business Ownership

Now, this one might throw you off a bit, but hear me out: skilled trades and small business owners are earning $200K too. And no, it’s not all about owning a massive company. Some people in fields like plumbing, electricians, or even construction management are making that kind of money.

Take, for example, a Redditor who’s a plumber. They’ve been in the business for years, but it wasn’t until they started their own company that things really took off. “I make $200K a year now,” they said. “It’s not just about the hourly wage anymore. Owning a business has really opened up the doors for higher earnings.” Sure, starting a business isn’t easy. It’s a lot of hard work and stress, but if you have the right mindset and work ethic, the rewards are huge. It’s an unconventional route to high earnings, but it’s definitely a viable one.

Creative Careers: Making It in the Arts

Okay, I’ll admit it — this one might sound a little far-fetched, but bear with me. Creative careers are, surprisingly, another way to hit the $200K mark. Whether you’re a successful content creator, a popular YouTuber, or someone running their own creative agency, earning a six-figure income is entirely possible. It’s not the norm, of course, but there are plenty of people who’ve turned their creative pursuits into highly profitable careers.

For instance, let’s think about digital marketing. A seasoned digital marketer, especially one working with high-profile clients, can easily pull in more than $200K a year. They’re the ones behind the ads, the strategies, and the campaigns that make companies millions. It’s not exactly the most traditional route to a high salary, but if you’ve got the right skill set and the willingness to take risks, it’s definitely within reach.

The Road Less Traveled: Entrepreneurship

Lastly, we can’t forget about entrepreneurship. Starting your own company is, obviously, a massive gamble. But it can also be extremely rewarding — both financially and personally. I’m not just talking about tech startups here (though, sure, that’s a route some people go down). I’m talking about launching businesses in areas like e-commerce, real estate investing, or consulting.

I mean, one Redditor talked about how they built a profitable online business selling custom products. "It wasn’t a quick jump," they said, “but after a few years, I hit the $200K mark. And honestly, it’s the flexibility and independence I love most.” Yes, entrepreneurship comes with its fair share of risks and stress, but the potential rewards — both financial and personal — can be pretty sweet.

Final Thoughts: It’s Possible, But It’s Not Easy

So, is making $200K a year in 2025 really that attainable? Well, yes and no. It’s definitely possible, but it’s not exactly easy. Whether you’re climbing the corporate ladder, venturing into the world of sales, starting your own business, or taking a creative leap, it requires a mix of hard work, strategy, and a bit of luck.

The key takeaway? There are a lot of ways to get there. You don’t have to follow the exact same path as everyone else. Sure, the traditional routes are there, but so are a bunch of unexpected, surprising options. With the right skills, mindset, and a willingness to take risks, $200K a year could be within your reach — it might just take a little creativity to get there.
