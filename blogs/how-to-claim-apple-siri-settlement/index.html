How to Claim the Apple Siri Settlement (And What You Need to Know)

It’s not every day that Apple pays you money. But if you've owned an Apple device in the last decade and you've had the odd, eerie moment where Siri seemed to wake up and “listen” without being called — well, this one's for you.

Apple is settling a class action lawsuit for $95 million, and eligible users can now file a claim to receive compensation. This article is your definitive guide to understanding the lawsuit, how to claim the apple siri settlement and determining if you qualify, and navigating the claim process from start to finish.

This settlement, also referred to as the lopez voice assistant class action settlement, covers claims involving Siri’s unintentional recordings and related privacy concerns.

Let’s break it all down — minus the legalese.

Why Is Apple Paying Out?

Let’s rewind.

In 2019, a report by The Guardian revealed that Apple had contractors listening to Siri recordings as part of a quality-control effort. That included unintended activations where Siri “mistakenly” started recording without a clear voice command — sometimes capturing sensitive conversations.

This led to a class-action lawsuit (Lopez v. Apple Inc.), which accused Apple of violating user privacy. While Apple denies any wrongdoing, it agreed to a $95 million settlement to resolve the matter.

Translation: You’re not imagining things — Siri really did get a little too curious.

Am I Eligible for a Payout?

Good question — let’s make sure you meet the requirements before we get your hopes up and guide you on how to claim the apple siri settlement.

You Are Eligible If:

  • You owned or used a Siri-enabled Apple device in the U.S. between September 17, 2014, and January 31, 2024.

  • You experienced at least one instance where Siri was accidentally triggered during a private or confidential conversation.

  • You can submit a claim form — even if you didn’t receive a postcard or email about the settlement.

You’re Likely Not Eligible If:

  • You never experienced a Siri mishap.

  • You used your Apple devices outside the U.S. exclusively.

  • You can't identify or recall your Apple devices used in that period.

⚠️ No proof of purchase is required — but you will need to provide device model names and (ideally) serial numbers.

How to Claim the Apple Siri Settlement

Alright, let’s walk through the actual process on how to claim the apple siri settlement.

Step 1: Go to the Official Website

Head over to lopezvoiceassistantsettlement.com. Make sure you’re on the official site — there are fake claim sites out there.

Step 2: Start a New Claim

Click “File Claim”. You’ll be asked for:

  • Full Name

  • Current Address

  • Email Address (preferably the one tied to your Apple ID)

Step 3: List Your Eligible Devices

You’ll need to enter:

  • Device Type (e.g., iPhone 11, MacBook Pro)

  • Approximate Year Purchased

  • Serial Number (if known, but not required)

There’s no need to upload receipts or proof of ownership.

Step 4: Confirm the Siri Trigger

You’ll be asked to attest that Siri activated unintentionally during a private conversation. This is legally binding — don't make it up.

Step 5: Submit Your Claim

Review all your details and hit submit. You’ll get a confirmation email once it’s accepted.

What Happens After You File?

Once you submit your claim, it enters the review phase. After the final approval hearing on August 1, 2025, payouts will begin.

Payment options may include:

  • Direct deposit

  • Check by mail

  • Digital payment (PayPal/Venmo)

Heads up: If the number of claims exceeds expectations, your payout could be lower than the $20/device cap.

Why the Settlement Actually Matters

Beyond the cash (which, sure, we all want), this case represents a broader shift. It’s one of the first major settlements acknowledging the privacy risk of voice assistants — and sets a precedent.

Think about it:

  • AI is getting more personal.

  • Smart devices are getting smarter — and more intrusive.

  • Companies are being held accountable (at least in theory) when your tech starts listening too much.

So whether you walk away with $20 or $100, this settlement nudges the needle forward on digital rights.

Got Questions? Here’s Your Siri Settlement FAQ

1. Do I need an email or postcard to file?

Nope. Those who were directly contacted may find it easier, but any eligible user can file manually on the settlement website.

2. What if I don’t know my device serial number?

That’s okay. You can still submit the model and approximate usage dates. Providing the serial number helps speed things up, though.

3. Is the payout guaranteed?

Not exactly. The maximum payout is $20/device (up to $100/person), but the actual amount depends on how many valid claims are filed.

4. Can I file for someone else (like a parent)?

Only if you are their legal representative (or executor of their estate, if deceased). You’ll need to submit additional documents in that case.

5. Is this just in the U.S.?

Yes — the settlement only applies to U.S.-based users and devices purchased/used within the U.S.

Don’t Miss Out

Claiming your portion of the Apple Siri settlement isn’t just about a few bucks — it’s about taking back a little control in a tech world that often operates in the shadows.

It’s quick. It’s free. And frankly, it’s the least Apple can do.

So if Siri ever woke up and made you wonder, “Wait, was she listening just now?” — go ahead and make your claim.
