CPT Internships That Pay Well (2025 List by Major)

April 20, 2025

Why CPT Internships Matter (More Than Ever)

For international students in the U.S. on F-1 visas, Curricular Practical Training (CPT) offers a critical opportunity to gain real-world professional experience that can have a lasting impact on your career. Beyond being just a resume booster, a CPT internship allows you to build essential skills, gain valuable industry exposure, and secure better job prospects after graduation. More than that, it can also lay the groundwork for longer-term work authorization in the U.S., which is essential for international students planning to stay and build their careers here.

However, it's important to understand that not all CPT internships are created equal. While some internships may offer minimal pay, others can offer substantial wages, with opportunities for growth and full-time job offers post-graduation. The key to making the most of your CPT experience lies in understanding how to identify high-paying opportunities and the factors that can make a difference in your internship search.

In this article, we’ll guide you through the importance of CPT internships in 2025, with a focus on which majors tend to secure higher wages, what you can do to improve your chances of landing a well-paying role, and how to leverage your CPT internship for long-term career success.

Understanding CPT: A Quick Refresher

Before we delve into the specifics of CPT internships, it’s important to first understand what CPT is and how it works. Curricular Practical Training is a type of temporary work authorization for F-1 students, allowing them to gain hands-on experience through internships, co-ops, or practicum that directly relate to their academic program.

There are a few key points to remember about CPT:

  • CPT must be directly related to your field of study.

  • It must be approved and documented by your school’s Designated School Official (DSO).

  • It can either be part-time (up to 20 hours per week) during the semester or full-time during official school breaks.

In some cases, students may have access to Day 1 CPT, meaning they can begin working as soon as they start their academic program. While this is legal under certain conditions, it comes with additional scrutiny, so it is vital to consult with your DSO and immigration attorney to ensure compliance.

How Much Do CPT Interns Get Paid in 2025?

The pay for CPT interns can vary widely based on several factors including your major, the location of the internship, the company you’re interning with, your level of experience, and the type of work you’re doing. For example, a technical role in a high-demand field like Computer Science or Data Science is likely to offer much higher pay compared to internships in non-technical fields like Business or Finance.

Here’s a breakdown of average hourly wages by major in 2025:

  • Computer Science: $30–$50/hour

  • Data Science: $35–$55/hour

  • Finance: $20–$38/hour

  • Business Analytics: $25–$40/hour

  • Engineering: $22–$45/hour

  • Healthcare/Public Health: $18–$35/hour

It's important to recognize that these figures are averages and can fluctuate based on the specific circumstances of the internship, such as the company’s size, industry, and geographic location. For instance, an internship at a tech giant in San Francisco could offer $50 per hour, whereas a similar role in a smaller city might offer significantly less.

Major-by-Major Breakdown of High-Paying CPT Internships in 2025

1. Computer Science / IT / Software Engineering

  • Top Roles: Software Engineer Intern, Web Developer Intern, Mobile App Developer, DevOps/SRE Intern

  • Average Pay: $30–$50/hour

  • Top Recruiters: Amazon, Google, Microsoft, Meta, Deloitte (Tech division), Infosys, Wipro, TCS

The tech industry remains a dominant player when it comes to high-paying internships. For international students with expertise in programming languages like Python, Java, or C++, or experience with cloud platforms such as AWS and Azure, the demand for your skills is high. Major tech firms actively seek interns with specialized knowledge in areas like backend development, mobile applications, and cloud infrastructure.

In order to maximize your chances of landing a high-paying internship, focus on building your personal portfolio. GitHub repositories showcasing personal projects are more valued by recruiters than your GPA, so make sure to upload your coding projects, contribute to open-source initiatives, and keep your skills up to date.

2. Data Science / AI / Machine Learning

  • Top Roles: Data Analyst Intern, Data Scientist Intern, Machine Learning Engineer Intern, BI Developer Intern

  • Average Pay: $35–$55/hour

  • Top Recruiters: IBM, Capital One, Meta, SAP, Zillow, various fintech firms

As more companies turn to data to guide their decision-making, data science has become one of the highest-paying fields for interns. The demand for interns with skills in machine learning, artificial intelligence, and data visualization tools like Tableau and Power BI is especially strong.

To prepare for these internships, familiarize yourself with key data science tools such as Python, SQL, and machine learning libraries (e.g., scikit-learn, TensorFlow). Interns who can demonstrate their ability to apply these technologies to solve real-world business problems are more likely to secure a lucrative internship.

3. Engineering (Electrical, Mechanical, Civil, and Industrial)

  • Top Roles: Embedded Systems Intern, Hardware Intern, Robotics Intern, Structural Analyst

  • Average Pay: $22–$45/hour

  • Top Recruiters: Intel, Qualcomm, NVIDIA, Tesla, Boeing, General Electric

Engineering students can also secure high-paying CPT internships, particularly in fields like electrical engineering and robotics, where specialized knowledge and hands-on experience with tools like MATLAB and AutoCAD are in high demand. Companies like Tesla, Intel, and NVIDIA often seek talented engineering interns who are proficient in hardware design, circuit analysis, and robotics.

Be sure to showcase any relevant project work or personal projects that demonstrate your hands-on capabilities. Including these on your resume and LinkedIn profile can help set you apart from other candidates.

4. Finance / Accounting / Investment

  • Top Roles: Financial Analyst Intern, Risk Analyst, Investment Research Intern, Tax Analyst

  • Average Pay: $20–$38/hour

  • Top Recruiters: JPMorgan Chase, Goldman Sachs, EY, BDO, Morningstar

For students pursuing degrees in Finance, Accounting, or Investment, securing a CPT internship with a top financial institution can be an excellent way to jumpstart your career. Interns in these fields often work on financial modeling, market analysis, and investment strategies, gaining exposure to high-level financial operations.

To be competitive in these internships, make sure to master Excel, financial analysis tools, and data visualization platforms like Tableau. Obtaining certifications like the CFA (Chartered Financial Analyst) can also increase your chances of landing a lucrative internship.

Bonus: Top CPT-Friendly Companies Across Industries

Not all companies are willing to navigate the complexities of CPT paperwork. However, there are several companies known for their willingness to hire international students and provide CPT opportunities. These companies have a good track record of sponsoring CPT work authorization and offer competitive internships:

  • Tech Firms: TCS, Infosys, Cognizant, Virtusa

  • Consulting Firms: Capgemini, Cognizant, Wipro

  • Finance and Investment Firms: JPMorgan Chase, Goldman Sachs, EY

When searching for opportunities, consider companies that specialize in consulting or tech, as they often have a greater need for talent and a more streamlined process for dealing with CPT. Research each company’s hiring process and verify their willingness to sponsor F-1 students before applying.

Final Thoughts: Take Action Now

Landing a high-paying CPT internship requires more than just submitting a generic resume. To maximize your chances of success, start early, target the right companies, and ensure that your resume reflects the relevant skills and projects that will make you stand out.

Whether you’re pursuing a technical field like Computer Science or a business-oriented field like Finance, there are opportunities available that can help you secure a well-paying internship. With the right strategy, networking, and proactive outreach, your CPT experience can be a stepping stone toward your dream job in the U.S.

Remember, your CPT internship is an investment in your career, and with the right approach, it can pay off significantly in the long run.
